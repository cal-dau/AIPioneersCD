George Boole

The Father of Boolean Logic

Early Life

  • Born in Lincoln, England, in 1815.
  • Self-taught in mathematics while working as a schoolteacher.
  • Became the first professor of mathematics at Queen’s College, Cork.

Boolean Logic

Boole developed a system of logic based on algebraic principles, now known as Boolean algebra.

  • Introduced logical operations AND, OR, and NOT.
  • Provided a mathematical foundation for binary computation.

Enduring Legacy

Boolean logic is the basis of modern digital circuits, programming, and artificial intelligence. George Boole’s work laid the mathematical foundations that power every computer today.
