Al-Khwarizmi

The Father of Algebra & Algorithms

Early Life

  • Born around 780 CE in Khwarazm (modern-day Uzbekistan).
  • Worked at the House of Wisdom in Baghdad, a center of learning in the Islamic Golden Age.
  • Studied mathematics, astronomy, and geography.

Founder of Algebra

His book Al-Kitab al-Mukhtasar fi Hisab al-Jabr wal-Muqabala introduced systematic solutions of linear and quadratic equations.

The word algebra comes from “al-jabr,” one of the operations he described.

Algorithms

His name gave rise to the word algorithm, a cornerstone concept in computer science and AI.

  • Created methods for calculation and problem-solving.
  • Laid the groundwork for logical step-by-step procedures.
  • Inspired modern programming and computation.

Enduring Legacy

Al-Khwarizmi’s works introduced algebra and algorithms to the world. His contributions continue to influence modern mathematics, computer science, and artificial intelligence.
