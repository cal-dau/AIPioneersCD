John McCarthy

The Father of Artificial Intelligence

Early Life & Education

  • Born in Boston, Massachusetts, in 1927.
  • Studied mathematics at Caltech and Princeton.
  • His early interests included logic, computation, and the philosophy of intelligence.

Dartmouth Conference, 1956

McCarthy organized the famous Dartmouth Conference, where he officially coined the term "Artificial Intelligence". This event marked the birth of AI as a research field.

Explore the Dartmouth Conference

The Lisp Programming Language

In 1958, McCarthy created Lisp, the second-oldest high-level programming language still in use.

  • Specially designed for AI research.
  • Introduced symbolic computation and recursion.
  • Still used in modern AI applications and symbolic reasoning systems.

Major Contributions

  • Co-developed the concept of time-sharing in computing.
  • Pioneered work in formalizing common sense reasoning.
  • Advanced the idea of AI as an independent academic discipline.
